What is Deforestation?

Deforestation refers to the large-scale removal of trees and forests, often resulting in the conversion of forested areas into agricultural land, urban development, or other non-forest uses. This process significantly impacts biodiversity, as it destroys the habitats of countless species and disrupts ecosystems. Additionally, deforestation contributes to climate change by releasing stored carbon dioxide into the atmosphere, reducing the earth's capacity to absorb CO2 through photosynthesis. The loss of forests also affects water cycles, soil health, and local climates, leading to increased erosion, flooding, and loss of arable land.





Causes of Desforestation:-

Deforestation is primarily caused by:
1. Agricultural Expansion: Clearing forests for crop production and livestock grazing.
2. Logging: Commercial logging for timber, paper, and fuel.
3. Urbanization: Expanding cities and infrastructure development leading to forest clearance.
4. Mining: Extraction of minerals and fossil fuels that often involves significant land clearing.
5. Infrastructure Development: Construction of roads, dams, and other infrastructure projects that encroach on forested areas.
6. Fire: Both natural and human-induced fires can lead to extensive forest loss.
7. Climate Change: Altered weather patterns can make forests more vulnerable to diseases and pests, contributing to deforestation.
