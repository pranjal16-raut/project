What is Biodiversity?

Biodiversity is the variety of all living things on Earth, including plants, animals, fungi, and even tiny organisms like bacteria. It also includes the different habitats where these species live, such as forests, oceans, and deserts, and the genetic differences within each species. This variety of life is important because each species plays a role in keeping ecosystems healthy and balanced. For example, plants produce oxygen, animals help pollinate plants, and microorganisms break down nutrients in the soil. Biodiversity helps ecosystems adapt to changes and provides us with essential resources like food, clean air, and water.
However, biodiversity is under threat due to human activities such as cutting down forests, polluting water, and contributing to climate change. These actions can destroy habitats and reduce the number of species, making ecosystems weaker and less able to support life. When biodiversity decreases, it can lead to a loss of essential resources and services that we depend on, like food and medicine. Protecting biodiversity through conservation efforts and sustainable practices is important to keep ecosystems balanced and ensure they can continue to support both wildlife and human communities.
